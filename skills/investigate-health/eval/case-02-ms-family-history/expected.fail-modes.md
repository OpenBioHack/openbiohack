# Failure modes this case tests

**Primary:** degree-of-relation mis-classification. Paternal grandmother is
**2nd-degree** (not 1st), shared DNA ~25%, MS risk-lift to ~0.4-1%
lifetime — not the ~10% figure that comes from mistakenly applying 1st-degree
relative rates.

Enforces:
1. Worked-example degree-of-relation table is applied — paternal grandmother
   classified as 2nd-degree.
2. Numerical lift is in the 0.3-1% range with a citation, not a memorised
   round number.
3. MRI is retained on the discriminator plan on cost-of-miss grounds (MS is
   irreversible if untreated and time-sensitive), not on inflated prior.
4. Prior is NOT inflated to ~10%.

Linked SKILL.md rule: register §"Worked example — degree-of-relation
magnitudes" + Step 7 §"Anything worth taking seriously even if it's not the
most likely".
