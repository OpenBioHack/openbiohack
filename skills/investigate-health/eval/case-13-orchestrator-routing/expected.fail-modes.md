# Failure modes this case tests

**Primary:** orchestrator lens selection on a **blended** presentation. The merge decision (A2) is that
investigate / optimize / check-in are *lenses on one continuum*, not separate products. A person who brings
both a floor to fix and a ceiling to raise must NOT be forced into a single "mode."

The case enforces that the orchestrator's orientation artifact:

1. Frames the situation on the **continuum** and explicitly **blends** the investigate + optimize lenses
   (names both), rather than collapsing to a hard dichotomy.
2. **Defers to the engine** (`investigate-health`) for the real investigation rather than shadow-running it
   in the orchestrator.
3. Reaches for the **optimize lens / forward-trace** for the recovery+cognition ceiling, while routing the
   bloating/crashes through the investigate thrust — and flags that an optimization assessment can surface a
   hidden Layer-1 problem (e.g. the low-normal ferritin) that switches a domain to investigation.
4. Stays non-directive (no "you should / must / need to", no outcome promises).

The MUST_NOT lines catch the old v1.1 "determine mode → you're in problem-fixing OR optimization" binary,
which the continuum design (orchestrator §1) deliberately replaces.

Linked rule: orchestrator `CLAUDE.md` §1 (continuum + 3 lenses) and §4 (routing — defer to the skills).
