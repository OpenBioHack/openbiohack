# Candidate levers that tend to be high-value

A reference catalogue, not a plan. The investigation's *offering* — and your clinician — decide what (if
anything) is worth trialing for *you*; this file just collects levers that come up often, with their honest
evidence status, so you have somewhere informed to start reading. Everything here is a **possibility to
consider and discuss with a qualified clinician**, then trial carefully using `n1-protocol.md`. Nothing here
is a recommendation, and "tends to be high-value across people" is not the same as "is right for you."

Two rules sit above this whole list:

- **Foundations first.** Per `optimization.md`, levers stacked on broken sleep/diet/movement give marginal
  returns at best. If Layers 1–2 aren't solid, that's where the leverage is — not here.
- **Check the lever against the person, not the average.** Genetics, current medications, existing
  conditions, and (especially) gut dysbiosis can flip a generally-safe lever into a poor fit. The
  delivery-vehicle check below and `/product-search` exist for exactly this.

---

## How to read the evidence labels

- **Strong, excellent safety** — replicated human trials / meta-analyses, wide safety margin. Low bar to a
  trial for most people with solid foundations.
- **Moderate, individually variable** — real human evidence, but response varies by genetics and baseline.
  Worth a tracked N=1.
- **Emerging, plausible mechanism** — mechanism is characterized, human data is thin. Experimental; strict
  N=1 with clear metrics only.

The evidence bar for *trialing* a low-risk reversible lever in a healthy person is reasonably relaxed
(favorable risk/benefit). The bar for *claiming it did something* is not — that still needs your own
before/after data.

---

## Strong evidence, excellent safety profile

Levers that tend to be worth discussing for almost anyone with solid foundations:

- **Creatine monohydrate** (literature range ~3–5 g/day) — cognitive and physical performance; one of the
  most-studied supplements, meta-analyses support both.
- **Vitamin D** (dose calibrated to blood work and to GC/VDR/CYP2R1 genetics; common target 40–60 ng/mL) —
  immune, mood, energy.
- **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** — inflammation, brain, cardiovascular; quality and dose matter, rTG form often
  preferred.
- **Magnesium** (glycinate or threonate, literature doses vary) — sleep, recovery, cognition, muscle.
- **Regular sauna** (~4+ sessions/week, ~20 min) — cardiovascular, recovery, mood; strong observational
  evidence.
- **Deliberate cold exposure** (brief, 1–3 min) — dopamine/norepinephrine, resilience; good evidence for
  acute effects.
- **Zone-2 cardio** (~3–4 h/week) — mitochondrial biogenesis, metabolic and cognitive health.

## Moderate evidence, good safety, individually variable

Worth a tracked trial; results vary:

- **NMN / NR** (literature ~250–500 mg/day) — NAD⁺ precursor; animal evidence strong, human evidence
  growing. **Important caveat — gut dysbiosis:** in SIBO / proximal-SI overgrowth, both NMN and NR can be
  salvaged by bacterial NAD⁺ pathways (e.g. *P. aeruginosa* PncC), which can feed the very organism that's
  a problem. For anyone with active gut dysbiosis, **nicotinamide** (host-preferred, ≤500 mg) is the safer
  candidate, and only after barrier work — and confirming no SIBO matters before considering NMN at all.
  This is exactly the kind of person-specific flip that makes a generally-fine lever a poor fit.
- **Ashwagandha** (KSM-66, ~600 mg/day) — cortisol modulation, stress resilience; multiple RCTs; watch
  thyroid effects.
- **Alpha-GPC** (~300–600 mg) — acetylcholine precursor, cognition; some RCT support; consider TMAO at
  higher doses.
- **Tongkat Ali** (~200–400 mg standardized) — hormonal markers in men; several RCTs.
- **Rhodiola rosea** (~200–400 mg) — fatigue, stress adaptation; multiple RCTs, extract quality varies.

## Emerging evidence, plausible mechanism, limited human data

Experimental — strict N=1 with clear metrics only:

- **Spermidine** — autophagy inducer; strong animal data, limited human trials, well-characterized mechanism.
- **Urolithin A (Mitopure)** — mitophagy; one clinical trial on muscle function; endogenous production from
  pomegranate varies by microbiome (so direct dosing may matter where conversion is impaired).
- **Apigenin** — possible CD38 inhibition (NAD⁺-sparing), sleep; mostly mechanistic + animal evidence.

## Levers that come up a lot but tend NOT to be worth it

- **Most "anti-aging" peptides** — insufficient safety data in healthy people; risk/benefit unfavorable.
- **High-dose antioxidants** (vitamin C >1 g, vitamin E) — may blunt exercise adaptation (hormesis); a whole-
  food diet covers antioxidant needs for healthy people.
- **Mega-dosing anything** — dose-response curves plateau or reverse; more is not better. The minimum
  effective dose is the goal.

## The delivery-vehicle check (easy to miss, often decisive)

Before considering *any* specific product, the *vehicle* matters as much as the active — and it's where a
generally-fine lever most often becomes a poor fit:

- **Liposomal forms** — phospholipid shells (phosphatidylcholine, soy lecithin) can feed gram-negative
  overgrowths (notably *P. aeruginosa* via PlcH/PlcN phospholipases). In confirmed dysbiosis, non-liposomal
  forms are the safer candidate (e.g. S-acetyl glutathione rather than liposomal glutathione; standard
  ascorbate rather than liposomal vitamin C).
- **Shared-facility allergens** — "made in a facility that also processes milk/wheat/soy/nuts" matters for
  protein-sensitive people; trace casein/whey can trigger reactions below the lactose level.
- **Capsule material** — pullulan/HPMC (vegan) vs gelatin; flag unusual excipients (rice flour, magnesium
  stearate sourcing) for grain/protein-sensitive people.
- **Targeted-release forms** — DRcaps and enteric coatings change *where* a supplement releases; this is
  decisive for things like lactoferrin (needs to reach the colon intact rather than be cleaved in the
  stomach).

For any specific product, `/product-search` runs this vehicle check automatically and cross-references the
full ingredient panel against documented constraints — use it rather than going by brand reputation or a
search-result title.
